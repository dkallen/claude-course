Module 4: Guided Exercise

Practice choosing and switching Codex permissions deliberately on a real repo.

Goal: Experience the difference between inspection, normal local editing, and boundary-crossing work.

Time estimate: 20–30 minutes

Part 1: Start Narrow

Put Codex in a read-only mode. Ask it to inspect the repo, summarize the structure, or explain a bug without making changes.

Part 2: Switch for Local Editing

Move to workspace-write and give Codex one narrow editing task inside the repo. Notice that this is already enough for most local development work.

Part 3: Observe a Boundary

Give Codex a task that would need network access or a write outside the workspace. Observe that this is a different category of permission from ordinary editing.

Part 4: Use the Surface Controls

  • In the app or IDE, find the permissions selector and switch modes.
  • In the CLI, use /permissions during a session if that is your surface.

Part 5: Decide Your Real Default

Write down the default you actually want for most sessions. For many users, the right answer is not full access. It is a narrower editing mode with deliberate escalation when needed.

Part 6: Capture What You Learned

Your final note should include:

  • what worked in read-only
  • what required workspace-write
  • what crossed a bigger boundary
  • what your preferred default should be
