Module 5: Guided Exercise

Turn one refined idea into a first-pass architecture before you write code.

Goal: Produce a short architecture brief with components, responsibilities, interfaces, and tradeoffs.

Time estimate: 35–45 minutes

Part 1: Bring Forward the Brief

Use the output from Module 4 or write a compact summary of your goals, problems, risks, and scope.

Part 2: Name the Major Components

Ask Claude for a coarse decomposition of the system. Push it to name clear responsibilities instead of vague layers.

Part 3: Draw the Boundaries

For each component, ask what it owns, what it should not own, and what information must cross into or out of it.

Part 4: Identify Interfaces

List the main interfaces or contracts. Keep this high-level. You are naming important promises, not designing every detail.

Part 5: Surface Tradeoffs

Have Claude compare your preferred design against at least one alternative and force the tradeoffs into the open.

Part 6: Capture the Architecture Brief

Your final artifact should include:

  • core components
  • responsibility of each component
  • key interfaces or contracts
  • main tradeoffs
  • open questions and risks
